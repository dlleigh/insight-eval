About Us

Insight Evaluation is a woman-owned small business. Its founder, Ellen Wilson, has more than 25 years of experience conducting research and evaluation in the health and human-services fields. She draws on a broad network of evaluators, data analysts, data visualization specialists, and other professionals, as needed, to assemble teams to address your research and evaluation needs. Based in Chapel Hill, NC, we work for programs locally, nationally, and around the world.

Approach

We take a collaborative approach to our work. We view you, our clients, as partners and involve you in all phases of the work, from study design to interpretation of results. Because every project is different, we tailor our approach to align with your unique goals. We are also sensitive to the challenges you face, and so we are flexible in responding to your needs as they evolve.

Values

Our core values guide all of our work.

  • Integrity: We maintain open and honest communication and ethical business and research practices.
  • Excellence: We are committed to the highest quality in everything we do.
  • Respect: We treat each other, our clients, and the communities where we work with respect.
  • Utility: We strive to produce results that will have a meaningful impact on programs and the communities they serve.
  • Social Justice: We are motivated by a desire to support social justice in the communities where we work.

Ellen Wilson, PhD
President

Ellen has spent the last 25 years conducting research and evaluations in a range of areas, including reproductive health, maternal and child health, teen pregnancy prevention, family planning, workforce development, healthy relationships, and fatherhood. She is motivated by the belief that programs can use evaluation and research findings to improve outcomes for the populations they serve.

Before founding Insight Evaluation, Ellen worked at RTI International and Child Trends, where she served as a senior researcher and led numerous research and evaluation projects. She has conducted evaluations of evidence-based programs and innovative new programs; developed and tested new program models; fielded surveys; conducted in-depth interviews, focus group discussions, and observations; and overseen the work of large research teams.

Ellen earned her PhD in Public Policy from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill and her BA from Yale University. She lives in Chapel Hill, NC, with her husband, two sons, and dog Poppy. In her spare time, she can be found playing tennis or walking Poppy.
